Daily mindfulness

What did you learn from your 'mindful conversation'? How did the other person find it?

Mindful conversation consists of:

  1. listening mindfully to the other person,
  2. looping, which means closing the loop of communication by demonstrating that you have really heard what the person is saying
  3. dipping, that is checking in with yourself, knowing how you are feeling about what you are hearing

I haven't done this formally but tried it informally in daily work situations. It feels like something that we all should do and to a degree a lot of people around me seem to.

It is nice to know the other person is listening intently and really hearing you. Repeating things back is extremely helpful to avoid conflicts that arise from simple misunderstandings. I think dipping is definitely the hardest part of mindful conversation.

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?

I like the idea of alternating between focused and open attention during meditation, I have used it during practice and will hopefully keep it up to sustain my practice.
